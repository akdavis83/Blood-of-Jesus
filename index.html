The blood of Jesus

(John 6:53) Then Jesus said unto them, Verily, verily, I say unto you, Except ye eat the flesh of the Son of man, and drink his blood, ye have no life in you.

The concept of totally consuming another living entity for the nourishment and sustainment of the body, that is the heart of what Jesus is trying to communicate in this intricate verse. We need Jesus more than natural sustainment, and He provides eternal sustainment starting at the point of decision. Meat and drink, if the belly is full and we have a decent place to rest and reset, that is happiness and a blessing. In the natural, when these things are taken care of life is easier and less stressful. Christ is our spiritual food and when we place Him in His rightful place He promises the provision of the natural things that are necessary that we seek. The blood of Jesus is powerful and mighty to save, heal, deliver, and cover our sins. The blood of Jesus is the scarlet thread woven throughout scripture, it is a flowing river of blood and water from His pierced side, and it is our very life force. As the body needs blood to continue and water to continue, so too need we the word of God to continue. Christ is the word made flesh, and His flesh and blood is the word we feed upon. His Spirit gives us life, and power to live the life He has called us to. You are what you eat is even more true in the realm of the Spirit. Jesus is now flesh and bones of a higher order, His glorified body has no need for blood any longer. He has poured out His blood into us as new creations, and His blood is poured out for us as sweet wine to drink. A table is set at the Masters banqueting feast, pull up a chair and dine to your hearts content.
